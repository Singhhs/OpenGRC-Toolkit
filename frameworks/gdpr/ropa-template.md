## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose

This document provides a template for maintaining Records of Processing Activities (RoPA) as required by GDPR Article 30. The RoPA serves as a comprehensive inventory of all personal data processing activities.

### 1.2 When RoPA is Required

Article 30 requires organizations to maintain a RoPA if:

- The organization has 250 or more employees, **OR**
- The processing is likely to result in risk to individuals, **OR**
- The processing is not occasional, **OR**
- The processing includes special category data or criminal conviction data

**Best Practice:** All organizations should maintain a RoPA regardless of size.

### 1.3 Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **DPO/Privacy Manager** | Maintain and update RoPA, coordinate with departments |
| **Department Heads** | Identify and document processing in their area |
| **Process Owners** | Provide accurate information about their processes |
| **IT** | Provide system and security information |

## 7. Maintenance Process

### 7.1 Review Schedule

| Activity | Frequency | Responsible |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Full RoPA review | Annually | DPO/Privacy Manager |
| Departmental updates | Quarterly | Department Heads |
| New process additions | As they occur | Process Owners |
| Accuracy verification | Bi-annually | DPO/Privacy Manager |

### 7.2 Update Triggers

Update the RoPA when:
- New processing activity begins
- Existing activity changes significantly
- New data categories collected
- New recipients added
- International transfers change
- Lawful basis changes
- Retention periods change
- Systems change
- Following DPIA or audit
