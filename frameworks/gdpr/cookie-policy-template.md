## 2. What Are Cookies?

### 2.1 Definition

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, smartphone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to website owners.

### 2.2 How Cookies Work

When you visit our Website:
1. Our server sends a small file to your browser
2. The browser stores the file on your device
3. When you return, the browser sends the cookie back
4. This helps us recognize you and remember your preferences

### 2.3 Types of Storage

| Technology | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Cookies** | Small text files stored by your browser |
| **Local Storage** | Browser storage for larger amounts of data |
| **Session Storage** | Data stored for the duration of a browser session |
| **Pixels/Beacons** | Tiny images that track page views and actions |
| **Scripts** | Code that can read and write data |

## 7. Legal Basis for Cookies

### 7.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

**Legal basis:** Legitimate interests (essential for Website operation)

We do not require consent for strictly necessary cookies as they are essential for the Website to function.

### 7.2 Non-Essential Cookies

**Legal basis:** Consent

We will only place non-essential cookies (analytics, functionality, marketing) after you have given your consent through our cookie banner.

### 7.3 ePrivacy Requirements

Under the ePrivacy Directive (and national implementing laws):
- Consent required before placing non-essential cookies
- Consent must be freely given, specific, informed
- Must be as easy to withdraw as to give
- Pre-ticked boxes do not constitute valid consent
