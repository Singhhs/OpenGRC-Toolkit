## GDPR Overview

### What is GDPR?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a comprehensive data protection law that came into effect on **25 May 2018**. It governs how organizations collect, process, store, and share personal data of individuals in the European Union (EU) and European Economic Area (EEA).

### Who Must Comply?

| Organization Type | When GDPR Applies |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| **EU-based organizations** | All processing of personal data |
| **Non-EU organizations** | When offering goods/services to EU residents |
| **Non-EU organizations** | When monitoring behavior of EU residents |
| **Processors** | When processing personal data on behalf of controllers |

---

## Key Principles (Article 5)

| Principle | Description | Key Actions |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency** | Process data lawfully and transparently | Privacy notices, lawful basis |
| **Purpose limitation** | Collect for specified, explicit purposes | Document purposes in RoPA |
| **Data minimization** | Adequate, relevant, limited to necessary | Review data collection |
| **Accuracy** | Keep personal data accurate and up to date | Correction processes |
| **Storage limitation** | Keep only as long as necessary | Retention schedules |
| **Integrity and confidentiality** | Appropriate security measures | Technical controls |
| **Accountability** | Demonstrate compliance | Documentation, training |

## Data Subject Rights

| Right | Article | Description | Your Action |
|-------|---------|-------------|-------------|
| **Right to be informed** | 13-14 | Know how data is used | Provide privacy notices |
| **Right of access** | 15 | Obtain copies of data | SAR procedure |
| **Right to rectification** | 16 | Correct inaccurate data | Update processes |
| **Right to erasure** | 17 | Request deletion | Deletion procedure |
| **Right to restrict processing** | 18 | Limit processing | Restriction capability |
| **Right to data portability** | 20 | Receive data in portable format | Export capability |
| **Right to object** | 21 | Object to certain processing | Objection handling |
| **Rights re: automated decisions** | 22 | Human review of automated decisions | Review process |

## Lawful Bases (Article 6)

| Lawful Basis | When to Use | Key Requirement |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Consent** | Marketing, non-essential cookies | Freely given, specific, withdrawable |
| **Contract** | Fulfilling agreements | Must be necessary for the contract |
| **Legal Obligation** | Required by law | Document the legal requirement |
| **Vital Interests** | Protect someone's life | Life-threatening situations only |
| **Public Task** | Official functions | For public authorities |
| **Legitimate Interests** | Your balanced interests | Conduct LIA, document assessment |

See [Lawful Basis Guide](./lawful-basis-guide.md) for detailed guidance.

## GDPR and ISO 27001

| GDPR Requirement | ISO 27001 Support |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Article 5 - Principles | Security supports confidentiality, integrity |
| Article 25 - Privacy by Design | A.8.25-8.31 Secure development |
| Article 30 - Records | A.5.9-5.11 Asset management |
| Article 32 - Security | A.8.1-8.34 Technical controls |
| Article 33-34 - Breach notification | A.5.24-5.28 Incident management |
| Article 35 - DPIA | Risk assessment process |

**Note:** ISO 27001 certification supports but does not guarantee GDPR compliance. GDPR has additional privacy-specific requirements.

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Administrative Fines

| Tier | Maximum Fine | Violations |
|------|--------------|------------|
| **Lower** | €10M or 2% global turnover | Controller/processor obligations |
| **Higher** | €20M or 4% global turnover | Principles, consent, rights, transfers |

### Other Consequences
- Reputational damage
- Regulatory orders
- Compensation claims
- Criminal liability (some jurisdictions)
