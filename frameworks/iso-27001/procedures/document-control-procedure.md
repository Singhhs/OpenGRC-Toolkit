# Document Control Procedure

## 1. Purpose
To ensure that information security management system (ISMS) documents are created, reviewed, approved, maintained, and updated in a controlled manner.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all documents (policies, procedures, standards, plans) required by ISO 27001 and the internal ISMS.

## 3. Responsibilities
- **Document Owner:** Creates and maintains the content.
- **Approver:** Reviews and authorizes the document (e.g., CISO, CTO).
- **All Employees:** Follow the latest approved versions.

## 4. Procedure
### 4.1. Creation and Update
- Use standard templates contained in the GRC Toolkit.
- Drafts are versioned (e.g., v0.1, v0.2).

### 4.2. Review and Approval
- Documents must be reviewed for adequacy.
- Approval must be documented (e.g., via Pull Request approval, email, or signature).

### 4.3. Version Control
- Approved documents are released as major versions (e.g., v1.0).
- A change log must be maintained at the beginning or end of the document.

### 4.4. Distribution and Access
- Documents are available to all relevant staff via [Intranet/Wiki/Repo].
- Access rights are controlled to prevent unauthorized modification.

### 4.5. Retention and Disposal
- Obsolete documents are identified and archived or destroyed to prevent unintended use.
- Retention periods are defined in the Data Retention Policy.
