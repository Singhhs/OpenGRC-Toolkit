## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for protecting [ORGANIZATION NAME]'s physical facilities, assets, and personnel from unauthorized access, damage, and interference.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All company facilities and offices
- Data centers and server rooms
- All employees, contractors, and visitors
- All physical assets and equipment

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 Secure Areas

#### 3.1.1 Area Classification

| Zone | Description | Access Level | Examples |
|------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| **Public** | Open to visitors | Unrestricted | Reception, lobby |
| **Controlled** | Employees and escorted visitors | Badge access | General office areas |
| **Restricted** | Specific authorized personnel | Badge + approval | Finance, HR, executive |
| **High Security** | Highly restricted | Badge + biometric/PIN | Data center, server room |

#### 3.1.2 Physical Security Perimeters

- All facilities shall have defined security perimeters
- Perimeter boundaries shall be clearly identifiable
- Walls shall extend from floor to ceiling
- External doors and windows shall be secured
- Alarm systems shall protect perimeter access points

### 3.2 Physical Entry Controls

#### 3.2.1 Access Control Systems

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Entry mechanism | Electronic access control (badge/card) |
| High-security areas | Multi-factor (badge + PIN or biometric) |
| Access logging | All entries and exits logged |
| Failed attempt alerts | After [3] failed attempts |
| Anti-passback | Enabled for controlled areas |

#### 3.2.2 Badge/Access Card Management

- Badges issued to all employees and regular contractors
- Badges shall display photo and name
- Badges shall be worn visibly at all times
- Lost badges reported immediately
- Badges deactivated upon termination/contract end
- Temporary badges for visitors

### 3.3 Visitor Management

#### 3.3.1 Visitor Procedures

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Pre-registration | Required for all expected visitors |
| ID verification | Government-issued photo ID |
| Sign-in/Sign-out | All visitors logged |
| Visitor badges | Visible, distinguishable from employee badges |
| Escort | Required in controlled and restricted areas |
| Badge return | Collected at departure |

#### 3.3.2 Visitor Access Restrictions

Visitors are NOT permitted in:
- Data centers
- Server rooms
- Secure storage areas
- Without escort in any controlled area

### 3.4 Delivery and Loading Areas

- Deliveries accepted only at designated areas
- Incoming materials inspected where possible
- Delivery area isolated from work areas
- Loading dock doors secured when not in use
- Courier access controlled

### 3.5 Securing Offices and Equipment

#### 3.5.1 Office Security

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Unattended offices | Locked when unoccupied |
| After hours | All areas secured |
| Sensitive areas | Additional locking mechanisms |
| Key management | Keys tracked and controlled |

#### 3.5.2 Equipment Security

| Equipment Type | Security Requirements |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Workstations | Cable locks for laptops, secured to desks |
| Servers | Locked cabinets in secure room |
| Network equipment | Secured cabinets |
| Removable media | Secured storage |

### 3.6 Clear Desk and Clear Screen

#### 3.6.1 Clear Desk Policy

At the end of each workday and when leaving workspace:
- Confidential/Restricted documents secured in locked storage
- Removable media secured
- Desks cleared of sensitive information
- Filing cabinets locked

#### 3.6.2 Clear Screen Policy

- Screens locked when leaving desk (even briefly)
- Automatic screen lock: Maximum [15] minutes
- Screens positioned to prevent unauthorized viewing
- Privacy screens for sensitive work areas

### 3.7 Data Center and Server Room Security

#### 3.7.1 Physical Controls

| Control | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Access | Limited to authorized personnel only |
| Entry | Multi-factor authentication |
| Logging | All access logged with time stamps |
| Monitoring | CCTV coverage, 24/7 |
| Visitor access | Pre-approved, escorted, logged |
| Environmental | Temperature, humidity controls |

#### 3.7.2 Access Authorization

Access to data center requires:
- Business justification
- Manager approval
- IT/Facilities approval
- Security briefing
- Access review: [Quarterly]

### 3.8 Environmental Protection

#### 3.8.1 Fire Protection

| Control | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Detection | Smoke/fire detectors in all areas |
| Suppression | Appropriate suppression systems |
| Extinguishers | Available and inspected regularly |
| Alarms | Audible throughout facility |
| Evacuation | Plans posted, drills conducted |

#### 3.8.2 Power and Utilities

| Control | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| UPS | Critical systems protected |
| Generator | Backup power for critical facilities |
| HVAC | Adequate for equipment requirements |
| Water detection | In areas with water exposure risk |

### 3.9 Equipment Protection

#### 3.9.1 Siting and Protection

- Equipment positioned to minimize unauthorized access
- Sensitive equipment in secure areas
- Equipment protected from environmental hazards
- Power supplies protected from failure

#### 3.9.2 Off-Premises Equipment

Equipment taken off-site must:
- Be approved for removal
- Have full disk encryption
- Be protected from theft and damage
- Not be left unattended in vehicles
- Be tracked by asset management

#### 3.9.3 Equipment Maintenance

- Only authorized personnel maintain equipment
- Vendor maintenance logged
- Escorted access for external maintainers
- Sensitive data removed before third-party maintenance

### 3.10 Cabling Security

- Power and network cabling protected from interference
- Critical cables in conduits or protected routes
- Cables labeled and documented
- Patch panels in secured locations

### 3.11 Secure Disposal

- Equipment sanitized before disposal or reuse
- Storage media destroyed or securely wiped
- Certificates of destruction obtained for sensitive data
- Asset tracking updated

### 3.12 CCTV and Monitoring

#### 3.12.1 Video Surveillance

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Coverage | Entry/exit points, secure areas, common areas |
| Recording | 24/7 recording |
| Retention | [30-90] days |
| Access | Restricted to security personnel |
| Signage | Notice of surveillance displayed |

#### 3.12.2 Monitoring

- Security guards or monitoring service (if applicable)
- Alarm monitoring 24/7
- Response procedures documented

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Facilities/Security Team
- Manage physical security controls
- Issue and revoke access badges
- Manage visitor procedures
- Conduct security rounds
- Respond to security incidents

### 4.2 IT Department
- Manage data center security
- Coordinate equipment security
- Support access control systems

### 4.3 All Employees
- Display badges at all times
- Challenge unknown persons
- Report security concerns
- Follow clear desk/screen policies
- Escort visitors in their area

### 4.4 Management
- Approve access requests
- Ensure compliance in their areas
- Report concerns

## 5. Incidents and Reporting

Report physical security incidents immediately:
- Unauthorized access attempts
- Tailgating/piggybacking
- Lost badges or keys
- Suspicious persons or packages
- Property damage or theft
- Environmental hazards

Contact: [Security/Facilities contact information]

## 6. Compliance

### 6.1 Monitoring

- Regular security patrols
- Access log reviews
- CCTV reviews
- Security audits

### 6.2 Violations

Violations may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Access revocation
- Disciplinary action
- Termination in serious cases
- Legal action if warranted
