## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of [ORGANIZATION NAME]'s information technology resources to protect the organization, its employees, and its partners from harm resulting from misuse of these resources.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary staff
- All IT resources owned, leased, or managed by [ORGANIZATION NAME]
- All devices connecting to [ORGANIZATION NAME] networks or systems

IT resources include but are not limited to:
- Computers (desktops, laptops, servers)
- Mobile devices (phones, tablets)
- Network infrastructure and internet access
- Email and messaging systems
- Software and applications
- Data and information assets
- Cloud services

## 3. Policy Statements

### 3.1 General Use

#### 3.1.1 Acceptable Use

IT resources are provided for business purposes. Users may:
- Use resources for authorized business activities
- Access systems and data relevant to their job function
- Use limited personal use where it does not interfere with work or violate policy

#### 3.1.2 Prohibited Activities

Users must NOT:

**Security Violations**
- Attempt to bypass security controls or access unauthorized systems
- Share passwords or authentication credentials
- Install unauthorized software or hardware
- Disable or interfere with security software
- Connect unauthorized devices to the network

**Illegal Activities**
- Engage in any illegal activity
- Download or distribute pirated software, music, or media
- Transmit or store illegal content
- Harass, threaten, or discriminate against others

**Harmful Activities**
- Introduce malware or malicious code
- Participate in denial-of-service attacks
- Intercept or monitor network traffic without authorization
- Attempt to compromise other systems or networks

**Inappropriate Use**
- Access or distribute pornographic or offensive material
- Conduct personal business activities
- Gamble using company resources
- Represent personal views as company positions

### 3.2 Email and Communication

#### 3.2.1 Acceptable Email Use
- Use professional language and tone
- Include appropriate signatures
- Protect confidential information
- Use encryption for sensitive data

#### 3.2.2 Email Prohibitions
- Sending confidential information to unauthorized recipients
- Opening suspicious attachments or links
- Using email for harassment or discrimination
- Forwarding chain letters or spam
- Auto-forwarding email to external accounts without approval

### 3.3 Internet Use

#### 3.3.1 Acceptable Internet Use
- Business research and communication
- Professional development and training
- Limited personal use during breaks

#### 3.3.2 Internet Prohibitions
- Accessing inappropriate or offensive websites
- Downloading unauthorized software
- Streaming excessive non-work media
- Using unauthorized file-sharing services
- Bypassing web filters or proxy servers

### 3.4 Mobile and Remote Use

- Secure devices with PINs, passwords, or biometrics
- Enable remote wipe capability
- Report lost or stolen devices immediately
- Use only approved applications for business data
- Connect only through secure, approved methods
- See [Mobile Device Policy](./mobile-device-policy.md) and [Remote Work Policy](./remote-work-policy.md)

### 3.5 Data Handling

#### 3.5.1 Data Protection
- Handle data according to its classification
- Do not store sensitive data on unauthorized locations
- Encrypt sensitive data in transit and at rest
- Dispose of data securely when no longer needed
- See [Data Classification Policy](./data-classification-policy.md)

#### 3.5.2 Data Prohibitions
- Copying sensitive data to personal devices without authorization
- Sharing data with unauthorized parties
- Transmitting sensitive data via insecure channels
- Removing data from the organization without approval

### 3.6 Software and Licensing

- Use only licensed and approved software
- Do not install personal software on company devices
- Report software license concerns to IT
- Do not copy or distribute software illegally

### 3.7 Social Media

- Do not disclose confidential information
- Clearly distinguish personal views from company positions
- Do not disparage the organization, employees, or partners
- Report security concerns discovered on social media
- Follow [ORGANIZATION NAME]'s social media guidelines

## 4. Monitoring and Privacy

### 4.1 Monitoring Notice

[ORGANIZATION NAME] reserves the right to:
- Monitor all use of IT resources
- Log network traffic and system access
- Inspect data stored on company systems
- Review email and communications
- Audit compliance with this policy

### 4.2 Privacy Expectations

Users should have no expectation of privacy when using [ORGANIZATION NAME] IT resources. Monitoring may occur without notice to:
- Ensure compliance with policies
- Investigate security incidents
- Support legal or regulatory requirements
- Maintain system performance

### 4.3 Personal Use Acknowledgment

Limited personal use is [permitted/not permitted]. If permitted:
- Must not interfere with job responsibilities
- Must not consume excessive resources
- Subject to all monitoring provisions
- User assumes all risk for personal activities

## 5. Reporting

Users must report:
- Security incidents or suspicious activity
- Lost or stolen devices
- Policy violations observed
- Phishing attempts or social engineering
- System vulnerabilities discovered

Report to: [IT Security / Help Desk] at [CONTACT INFORMATION]

## 6. Compliance

### 6.1 Acknowledgment
All users must acknowledge this policy:
- Upon hiring or engagement
- Upon significant policy updates
- During annual security awareness training

### 6.2 Violations
Violations may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Suspension of access privileges
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Legal action where appropriate
- Liability for damages caused

### 6.3 Reporting Violations
Report suspected violations to:
- Line manager
- IT Security: [CONTACT]
- HR: [CONTACT]
- Anonymous hotline: [IF AVAILABLE]

## 7. Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy require:
- Written request with business justification
- Risk assessment
- Approval from [IT Security Manager/CISO]
- Documentation and review date
