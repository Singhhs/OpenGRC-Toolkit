## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes requirements for the protection of personal data processed by [ORGANIZATION NAME] to ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws and regulations, including GDPR where applicable.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- All personal data processed by [ORGANIZATION NAME]
- All employees, contractors, and third parties processing personal data
- All systems and processes handling personal data
- All locations where personal data is processed

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Personal Data** | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person |
| **Special Category Data** | Personal data revealing racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, sex life/orientation |
| **Data Subject** | An identified or identifiable natural person whose data is processed |
| **Processing** | Any operation performed on personal data (collection, storage, use, disclosure, deletion) |
| **Data Controller** | Entity determining purposes and means of processing |
| **Data Processor** | Entity processing data on behalf of a controller |
| **DPO** | Data Protection Officer |

## 4. Data Protection Principles

[ORGANIZATION NAME] shall ensure personal data is:

### 4.1 Lawfulness, Fairness and Transparency
- Processed lawfully with valid legal basis
- Processed fairly without detriment to data subjects
- Processing is transparent to data subjects

### 4.2 Purpose Limitation
- Collected for specified, explicit, legitimate purposes
- Not processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes

### 4.3 Data Minimization
- Adequate, relevant and limited to what is necessary
- Only data required for the purpose is collected

### 4.4 Accuracy
- Accurate and kept up to date
- Inaccurate data rectified or erased without delay

### 4.5 Storage Limitation
- Kept only as long as necessary for the purposes
- Retention periods defined and enforced

### 4.6 Integrity and Confidentiality
- Processed securely with appropriate technical and organizational measures
- Protected against unauthorized or unlawful processing, loss, destruction, or damage

### 4.7 Accountability
- Demonstrate compliance with these principles
- Maintain appropriate documentation

## 5. Lawful Basis for Processing

### 5.1 Valid Legal Bases

Personal data shall only be processed under one of the following legal bases:

| Legal Basis | Description | When to Use |
|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| **Consent** | Clear, affirmative consent from data subject | Marketing, optional services |
| **Contract** | Necessary for contract with data subject | Employment, customer services |
| **Legal Obligation** | Required by law | Tax records, regulatory reporting |
| **Vital Interests** | Protect life of data subject or another | Emergency situations |
| **Public Interest** | Necessary for public interest task | Public authorities |
| **Legitimate Interests** | Necessary for legitimate interests (balanced against data subject rights) | Business operations, security |

### 5.2 Special Category Data

Additional conditions required for special category data:
- Explicit consent
- Employment/social security law obligations
- Vital interests (where consent cannot be given)
- Legal claims
- Public health

### 5.3 Documentation

For all processing activities, document:
- The lawful basis relied upon
- Justification for that basis
- Any consent obtained

## 6. Data Subject Rights

[ORGANIZATION NAME] shall facilitate the following rights:

| Right | Description | Response Time |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Right to be Informed** | Privacy notices, transparency | At collection |
| **Right of Access** | Copy of personal data and processing info | 30 days |
| **Right to Rectification** | Correct inaccurate data | 30 days |
| **Right to Erasure** | Delete data in certain circumstances | 30 days |
| **Right to Restrict Processing** | Limit processing in certain circumstances | 30 days |
| **Right to Data Portability** | Receive data in portable format | 30 days |
| **Right to Object** | Object to certain processing | 30 days |
| **Rights re: Automated Decisions** | Human review of automated decisions | 30 days |

### 6.1 Handling Requests

1. Verify identity of requestor
2. Log request with date received
3. Assess and respond within timeframe
4. Document response and rationale
5. Escalate complex requests to DPO

## 7. Privacy Notices

### 7.1 Requirements

Privacy notices must include:
- Identity and contact details of controller
- Contact details of DPO (if applicable)
- Purposes and legal basis for processing
- Legitimate interests (if relied upon)
- Recipients or categories of recipients
- International transfer details
- Retention periods
- Data subject rights
- Right to withdraw consent
- Right to lodge complaint
- Whether provision is statutory/contractual
- Automated decision-making details

### 7.2 When to Provide

- At time of collection (direct collection)
- Within reasonable period (indirect collection)
- Before new processing purposes

## 8. Data Protection by Design and Default

### 8.1 Privacy by Design

All new projects, systems, and processes must:
- Consider privacy from the outset
- Implement appropriate safeguards
- Minimize data collection
- Complete privacy impact assessment if required

### 8.2 Privacy by Default

- Only necessary data processed by default
- Data not made public by default
- Retention minimized by default
- Access restricted by default

## 9. Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

### 9.1 When Required

DPIA required when processing likely to result in high risk:
- Systematic and extensive profiling with significant effects
- Large scale processing of special categories
- Systematic monitoring of public areas
- New technologies
- Large scale processing affecting many data subjects

### 9.2 DPIA Process

1. Describe the processing
2. Assess necessity and proportionality
3. Identify and assess risks
4. Identify measures to mitigate risks
5. Sign off and integrate outcomes
6. Keep under review

## 10. Records of Processing Activities

### 10.1 Controller Records

Maintain records including:
- Name and contact details of controller/DPO
- Purposes of processing
- Categories of data subjects and personal data
- Categories of recipients
- International transfers and safeguards
- Retention periods
- Security measures description

### 10.2 Processor Records

Maintain records including:
- Name and contact details of processor and controller
- Categories of processing
- International transfers and safeguards
- Security measures description

## 11. International Data Transfers

### 11.1 Transfer Restrictions

Personal data shall only be transferred outside [EEA/UK/jurisdiction] where:
- Adequacy decision exists
- Appropriate safeguards in place (SCCs, BCRs)
- Derogation applies (explicit consent, contract necessity, etc.)

### 11.2 Safeguards

Approved transfer mechanisms:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
- Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)
- Approved certification mechanisms
- Approved codes of conduct

### 11.3 Transfer Documentation

Document for each transfer:
- Data categories
- Destination country
- Transfer mechanism
- Risk assessment (Transfer Impact Assessment)

## 12. Data Processors

### 12.1 Processor Requirements

Only use processors that:
- Provide sufficient guarantees
- Act only on documented instructions
- Ensure personnel confidentiality
- Implement appropriate security
- Assist with data subject requests
- Assist with compliance obligations
- Delete or return data on termination
- Allow and contribute to audits

### 12.2 Processor Contracts

Contracts must specify:
- Subject matter and duration
- Nature and purpose of processing
- Type of personal data
- Categories of data subjects
- Obligations and rights of controller
- All requirements from 12.1

## 13. Data Breaches

### 13.1 Breach Definition

Personal data breach: security breach leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to personal data.

### 13.2 Breach Response

1. **Contain** - Stop the breach
2. **Assess** - Determine data and individuals affected
3. **Notify Authority** - Within 72 hours if risk to individuals
4. **Notify Individuals** - Without undue delay if high risk
5. **Document** - Record all breaches regardless of notification

### 13.3 Notification Requirements

**To Supervisory Authority (within 72 hours):**
- Nature of breach
- Categories and numbers affected
- DPO contact details
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken/proposed

**To Data Subjects (if high risk):**
- Clear, plain language
- Nature of breach
- DPO contact details
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken/proposed

- Review and dispose of data when no longer needed

## 15. Roles and Responsibilities

### 15.1 Data Protection Officer (DPO)

*[If applicable - required for public authorities, large-scale monitoring, large-scale special category processing]*

Responsibilities:
- Inform and advise on data protection obligations
- Monitor compliance
- Advise on DPIAs
- Cooperate with supervisory authority
- Act as contact point

### 15.2 Senior Management
- Ensure adequate resources for data protection
- Demonstrate commitment to compliance
- Approve data protection policies

### 15.3 Department Managers
- Ensure compliance in their areas
- Identify processing activities
- Report data protection concerns

### 15.4 All Staff
- Process personal data per policy
- Report breaches and concerns
- Complete data protection training
- Maintain confidentiality

## 16. Training and Awareness

- All staff complete data protection training on joining
- Annual refresher training required
- Role-specific training for high-risk processing
- Training records maintained

## 17. Compliance Monitoring

### 17.1 Monitoring Activities

- Regular compliance audits
- Processing activity reviews
- DPIA reviews
- Breach analysis
- Training completion tracking

### 17.2 Reporting

- Regular reports to senior management
- Breach reports as required
- Annual compliance summary
