# Press Statement Templates

Templates for media communications during security incidents.

**IMPORTANT:** All media communications must be approved by Legal, Communications, and Executive leadership before release.

---

## Initial Press Statement

### Holding Statement (Before Full Details Known)

**FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE**

**[ORGANIZATION NAME] Statement Regarding Security Incident**

[CITY, STATE] - [DATE]

[ORGANIZATION NAME] is aware of a security incident affecting [general description - our systems / some customer data / our website]. We are actively investigating and have engaged leading cybersecurity experts to assist.

We take the security of [our customers' / our partners' / data] seriously. Upon detecting this incident, we immediately took steps to [contain the situation / secure our systems / protect affected data].

We are working diligently to understand the full scope of this matter and will provide updates as our investigation progresses.

[If applicable: We have notified law enforcement and are cooperating fully with their investigation.]

[NAME]
[TITLE]

## Media Call/Interview Talking Points

### Key Messages (Stick to These)

1. **We take security seriously**
   - Security is a top priority
   - We invest significantly in protecting data
   - We deeply regret this incident

2. **We acted quickly**
   - Discovered on [DATE]
   - Immediately engaged experts
   - Took swift containment actions

3. **We're being transparent**
   - Notifying affected individuals
   - Working with authorities
   - Providing regular updates

4. **We're supporting affected individuals**
   - [Credit monitoring offer if applicable]
   - Dedicated support resources
   - Clear guidance on protective steps

5. **We're preventing future incidents**
   - Enhanced security measures
   - Ongoing investment in security
   - Learning from this incident

### Bridging Phrases

- "What I can tell you is..."
- "What's most important here is..."
- "Let me be clear about..."
- "Our focus right now is..."

### Things to Avoid

- Speculation about attackers or methods
- Specific technical details
- Blame (vendors, employees, etc.)
- Minimizing the incident
- Guarantees ("This will never happen again")
- Discussing legal matters
- Comparing to other companies' breaches

|--------|--------|
